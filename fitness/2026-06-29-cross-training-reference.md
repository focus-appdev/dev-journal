# Cross-Training Reference — Skiing, Hiking & Tennis

**Date:** 2026-06-29

A quick reference for exercises that cross-train well for my three target
activities — skiing/snowboarding, hiking, and tennis.

---

## Skiing / Snowboarding
*Focus: leg endurance and lateral stability*

| Exercise | Why |
|----------|-----|
| Wall sits | Mimics sustained quad burn of holding a ski position |
| Lateral band walks | Hip abductors are key for edge control |
| Bulgarian split squats | Balance and unilateral strength |
| Box jumps | Explosive power for absorbing terrain changes |
| Pallof press | Anti-rotation core stability on uneven snow |

---

## Hiking
*Focus: posterior chain, ankle stability, cardio base*

| Exercise | Why |
|----------|-----|
| Step-ups with weight | Directly mimics uphill climbing |
| Romanian deadlifts | Hamstrings and glutes for descents (saves the knees) |
| Calf raises | Ankle fatigue is real on long trails |
| Incline treadmill walks | Cardio specificity without a bike |

---

## Tennis
*Focus: explosive lateral movement and rotational power*

| Exercise | Why |
|----------|-----|
| Lateral lunges | Court coverage |
| Med ball rotational throws | Groundstroke power comes from the hips |
| Agility ladder drills | Footwork and reaction speed |
| Face pulls / band external rotation | Shoulder injury prevention |

---

## The One Exercise That Covers All Three

**Bulgarian split squats** (or step-ups) — single leg work builds the
balance, stability, and unilateral strength that carries over to all
three activities.

---
